The Nintendo DS Era
2004-2011
 
Next up on Nintendo’s list was yet another handheld system. But this next system was Nintendo’s next giant step in the gaming industry. The Nintendo DS was released on November 21st, 2004 for $149.99. This handheld looked much like their GameBoy Advance SP from a few years back, having the exact same clamshell design to it. The only difference was that the DS had something new to not just the gaming world, but the tech world as a whole. It was the first major use of the touchscreen, which was just a screen that you could use your hands and fingers to do stuff on, for example to draw or press buttons. It was incredible, but the DS sold a pretty low amount at first with just about 18 million worldwide. But the DS had a lot a variants, just like the GameBoy did. This in turn brought in even more sales, especially the DS Lite which brought in over 93 million sales. Altogether, the DS sold a little over 154 million copies worldwide, Nintendo’s best selling console and the 2nd best selling in the world behind the PlayStation 2. Along with that the DS had fan favorite titles, especially the New Super Mario Bros. series, which is still going to this day, and many Pokemon games like Pokemon: Diamond, Pokemon: Pearl, Pokemon: White, Pokemon Black, and even a few more. Later on though, in 2011, the Nintendo 3DS was released which was an entirely new take on the DS to the point of being classified as a completely new system. Its retail price was $249.99, a whole $100 more than the DS was, but it didn’t stop people from buying. The selling point of the 3DS was having an even better graphical system and the incredibly 3D effect, which was mostly used once and never again by most people since it slowed down the games a lot. It was, surprisingly, Nintendo’s least selling handheld at the time, only around 69 million copies sold when including all the different variants it had. And then after that, the 3DS released the 2DS, a cheaper way to get the 3DS, the only thing different about it being the design and no 3D feature on it. Only just recently, around the Nintendo Switch’s release, did the Nintendo DS Era end, stopping all production of games for it. After a whopping 12 years, the DS and 3DS sold around 223 million copies worldwide and was undoubtedly their most amazing and game changing console to date.
