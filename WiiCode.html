The Nintendo Wii
2006
 
After the DS, Nintendo’s best selling handheld, Nintendo rolled out the Wii, their best selling home console. It was released in 2006 for the same price as a 3DS, $249.99. This is when Nintendo started to stretch out from what their target demographic was. Instead of aiming it at strictly children, Nintendo slowly began to make themselves more targeted at everyone. And the Wii was the perfect way to do it. The Wii was mostly meant for casual play and that is what really brought people in. But along with that the Wii was the first ever system to correctly use motion controls which interested people even more. It was just overall a great system and allowed for families to entertain themselves rather than just a child. The Wii also brought back games for many series’ that had been absent for a while. A few of these games were New Super Mario Bros: Wii, Donkey Kong Country Returns, Kirby’s Return to Dreamland, The Legend of Zelda: Skyward Sword, Super Smash Bros: Brawl, and quite a lot more. But it also had new games that had sold even better and were liked more than all of those. The biggest one of those being Wii Sports, which surprisingly still has not seen a return since the WIi U days. Near the end of its lifetime, the Wii sold just a bit more than 100 million copies and was discontinued in 2013.
