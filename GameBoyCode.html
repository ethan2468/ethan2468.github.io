The Game Boy
1989
 
The Game Boy was again not another home console of Nintendo’s, but a handheld similar to the Game & Watch. The Game Boy was released in 1989 and was their first of many handhelds to take cartridges, and is also what popularized Tetris, being one of the handheld’s first feature titles. The Game Boy was an even better seller than the NES, selling over 120 million copies in its lifetime. The Game Boy had many different variants that were released, the biggest versions being The Game Boy Color in 1998, The Game Boy Advance in 2001, and The Game Boy Advance SP; the design of it giving inspiration for the future handheld system, the Nintendo DS. The Game Boy launched as $89.99 for the retail price and with games such as Alleyway, Baseball, Super Mario Land, Yakuman, and North America getting Tetris and Tennis.
