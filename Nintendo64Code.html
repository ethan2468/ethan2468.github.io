Nintendo 64
 
 
The Nintendo 64
1996
 
It seems after The Virtual Boys awful reputation, Nintendo kicked it into high gear with what many people will say is their best console to date. The Nintendo 64 was released in 1996 for $199.99 USD. At the time, Nintendo was starting to gain rivals in the gaming industry, mainly being SEGA and Sony. The Nintendo 64 sold pretty well with a nice 32 million copies sold, which was less than both SEGA’s and Sony’s consoles at the time, being the Sony Playstation and the SEGA Dreamcast and Genesis. But even with that, The Nintendo 64 has some of the biggest and most favorite video games Nintendo has ever released. Examples of this are Super Mario 64, The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time, The Legend of Zelda: Majora’s Mask, Banjo-Kazooie, Super Smash Bros., GoldenEye-007, and many, many more. Overall, The Nintendo 64 has to be one of Nintendo’s most important consoles, being their first to include fully 3D graphics. The Nintendo 64 was discontinued in 2002.
